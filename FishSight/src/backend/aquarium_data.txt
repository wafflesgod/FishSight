=== PLANT CARE: EPIPHYTES & FLOATERS ===
1. Anubias (Various Species): Epiphytic plant. Must be attached to wood or rock. Do not bury the rhizome in the substrate or it will rot. Thrives in low light and is very hardy.
2. Amazon Frogbit: Floating plant with long trailing roots. Excellent for absorbing excess nitrates. Requires low surface agitation; the leaves will rot if constantly splashed with water.
3. Mosses (Java, Christmas): Low light, slow-growing. Great hiding spots for shrimp fry. Attach to hardscape.
4. Cabomba: Fast-growing stem plant. Requires moderate to high light. Great for the background and provides excellent cover for small fish.

=== PLANT CARE: HEAVY ROOT FEEDERS ===
1. Amazon Sword (Echinodorus amazonicus): Large background plant. Heavy root feeder. Requires nutrient-rich substrate or regular insertion of root tabs beneath its roots to prevent yellowing leaves.

=== FISH SPECIES: PEACEFUL COMMUNITY ===
1. Guppies (Poecilia reticulata): Hardy livebearers. Top to middle water column dwellers. They breed rapidly, so monitor tank capacity.
2. Cherry Barbs (Puntius titteya): Peaceful schooling fish. Best kept in groups of 6 or more. They display the best red coloration in densely planted tanks with darker substrates.
3. Pygmy Corydoras (Corydoras pygmaeus): Miniature bottom-dwelling catfish. Peaceful and must be kept in schools (6+). Requires smooth substrate (like sand or smooth soil granules) to prevent damage to their sensitive barbels.

=== FERTILIZERS & SUPPLEMENTS ===
1. Seachem Flourish Excel: A bioavailable organic carbon source. Acts as a mild algaecide. Dose carefully according to the bottle; overdosing can harm sensitive fish, shrimp, and melt certain plants like Vallisneria.
2. Seachem Flourish Phosphorus: Macronutrient supplement. Phosphorus deficiency in plants often presents as stunted growth or dark green/purplish leaves. 
3. ARK Liquid Fertilizer: An all-in-one liquid fertilizer providing essential micro and macronutrients for planted tanks. Best dosed weekly or split into smaller daily doses for stability.

=== TANK MAINTENANCE: FEEDING ===
1. Fluval Bug Bites: High-quality food utilizing Black Soldier Fly Larvae as the main ingredient. Excellent for mimicking the natural insect-based diet of small community fish.
2. Dennerle Fish Food: Premium staple food, highly digestible, which helps reduce fish waste and maintain water quality.
3. Feeding Rule: Feed only what the fish can consume in 1-2 minutes. Overfeeding leads to ammonia spikes, snail outbreaks, and algae blooms.

=== TANK SIZING & DIMENSIONS ===
1. Nano Tanks (e.g., 45x30x30 cm): Holds roughly 40 liters (10 gallons). Ideal for single Bettas, shrimp colonies, or small schools of micro-rasboras/Pygmy Corydoras. 
2. Standard Small Tanks (e.g., 60x30x30 cm): Holds roughly 54 liters (15 gallons). Offers more stability. Great for a mixed community of Cherry Barbs, Guppies, and bottom dwellers.
